## Context

Scribular is a local-first AI therapy note generator (desktop app, on-device audio processing). Pre-revenue, 4 trial users, 130 sessions recorded. Our ICP is solo and small-group mental health practitioners in the US. Current GTM is direct 1:1 outreach (cold email, LinkedIn, Facebook groups) — no paid acquisition.

We're considering building a free, lightweight web-based dictation tool on scribular.com that helps therapists get their overdue notes done — a "notes crisis" tool. The idea: capture therapists at their pain point (behind on documentation) and introduce them to Scribular naturally, without it feeling like a bait-and-switch.

We already have a prompt-scrubbing tool (Prompt Guard) that could provide privacy protection in the web version.

## What You Need

- Examples of free web tools that successfully drove signups for a paid desktop/SaaS product — especially in niche professional markets
- Guidance on scope: how minimal can the free tool be while still delivering enough value to build trust and create a natural upgrade path?
- Red flags or failure modes: when does a free tool cannibalize the paid product vs. feed it?
- Any experience with "capture at the pain point" funnels in high-trust verticals (healthcare, legal, financial)

## What You've Already Tried

- Direct 1:1 cold email outreach — 22% reply rate, good for conversations but doesn't scale
- LinkedIn 12-post philosophy arc — builds credibility but slow conversion path
- Facebook group engagement — education-first replies, some inbound interest but low volume
- Paid Meta ads — paused per PG's advice to focus on direct conversations first
- We have not built or launched any free web tool yet — this is at the brainstorm stage
