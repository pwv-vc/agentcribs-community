## Context

Scribular is a local-first AI therapy note generator. Pre-revenue, 4 trial devices active, 0 paid conversions, 130 sessions recorded. Our website (scribular.com) has no social proof — no testimonials, no logos, no case studies.

Our ICP is solo and small-group mental health practitioners. This is a high-trust vertical where therapists evaluate tools cautiously, often relying on peer recommendations. The absence of any social proof on our site is a known conversion gap (identified via customer journey mapping).

We have a small number of trial users who are actively using the product but haven't converted to paid yet. Some have given positive verbal feedback in discovery calls.

## What You Need

- Tactics for getting usable testimonials from trial users (not yet paying customers) — what to ask, when to ask, what format works
- Creative alternatives to traditional testimonials when you have <10 users (usage stats, specific outcomes, founder story, third-party validation)
- Examples of effective social proof on SaaS landing pages at the pre-revenue or very-early-revenue stage
- What NOT to do — social proof attempts that backfire or feel inauthentic at small scale

## What You've Already Tried

- Discovery calls with trial users — received positive verbal feedback but haven't formalized any into testimonials
- Customer journey map exercise — identified "trust gap" at the website evaluation stage as a key blocker
- Philosophy-first LinkedIn content — builds founder credibility but doesn't transfer as social proof on the product site
- We have not yet asked any user directly for a testimonial or review
